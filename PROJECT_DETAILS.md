# Key Concepts

## Interactive Map

In order to give the feel of actually exploring a world that exists, I want a map that allows players to traverse the world. This means an overarching "world" map, where clicking a city takes you the map for that city, and clicking a region might either take you to a map of that region, or display an information page for that part of the city.

I feel like this will give a true sense of things actually existing where they are said to be in the story, and also encourages players to engage with more layers of fiction. Instead of "gah, I can't remember the name of the faction we talked to yesterday", a player might say "gah, I can't remember, but I know it was in Crate Town. Let me go there real quick and check the factions. Oh yeah, it was The Rusty Knives, and we were chatting with Slate, the second-in-command."

## Entity Browser

Instead of only being able to navigate through the map, I think a nice relatively-flashy browser that enables visitors to swipe through known factions/cities/characters and easily view the relevant data page would be useful.

## Themes

You can set a lot of mood about a world with proper choice of font and colors. I think enabling World Builders to choose colors and font would be an easy slam dunk. For example, my world set in the universe of Warhammer 40k could use terminal font with a green and black color scheme, giving the feeling of dataslates.

## Secrets

Certain parts of the world shouldn't be known to the most superficial of visitors. Those who have explored and been a part of the worlds evolution might naturally know more, and by inputting the proper codewords they can unlock extra details known only to them and others with that level of access.
